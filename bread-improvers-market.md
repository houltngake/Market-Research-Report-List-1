<p><p>The bread improvers market comprises various types, including emulsifiers, enzymes, oxidizing agents, and reducing agents. Emulsifiers enhance dough stability and texture, promoting a uniform crumb structure. Enzymes, such as amylases, improve fermentation and shelf life by breaking down starches. Oxidizing agents strengthen gluten, ensuring better dough elasticity and volume, while reducing agents weaken gluten, making dough more extensible. These components are essential for achieving desired bread quality, influencing characteristics like taste, texture, and appearance.</p></p>
