<p><p>The calcined kaolin market is categorized primarily into two types based on their properties: absorbency and inhibitory. Absorbent calcined kaolin is utilized for its moisture-wicking capabilities, commonly found in applications such as cosmetics, paints, and sealants. In contrast, inhibitory calcined kaolin serves as a functional additive, effectively enhancing the performance of products by reducing friction or providing thermal insulation. Both types are valued for their unique characteristics, driving demand across various industries, including plastics, paper, and ceramics.</p></p>
