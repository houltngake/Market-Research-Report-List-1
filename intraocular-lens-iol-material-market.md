<p><p>The intraocular lens (IOL) material market features various types, each with distinct properties. Polymethyl Methacrylate (PMMA) offers rigidity and durability, ideal for established applications. Silicone lenses provide flexibility and biocompatibility, enhancing optical performance. Hydrophilic acrylic or hydrogel lenses absorb water, improving comfort and adaptability within the eye. Hydrophobic acrylic lenses resist moisture, promoting clearer vision and reducing complications. Each material caters to specific patient needs and surgical techniques, influencing the growing IOL market's dynamics.</p></p>
