<p><p>The solar cell market comprises various module types, each with distinct characteristics. Mono-Si (monocrystalline silicon) modules are known for high efficiency and space-saving designs. Multi-Si (multicrystalline silicon) modules are more cost-effective but typically less efficient. CdTe (cadmium telluride) modules are known for lower production costs and good performance in low-light conditions. CIGS (copper indium gallium selenide) modules offer flexibility and high absorption. a-Si (amorphous silicon) modules are lightweight and suited for niche applications. Other market segments include emerging technologies and hybrid systems.</p></p>
