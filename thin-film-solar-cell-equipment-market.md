<p><p>The thin film solar cell equipment market is categorized into three main technologies: Amorphous Silicon (A-Si), Cadmium Telluride (CdTe), and Copper Indium Gallium Selenide (CIGS). A-Si technology utilizes a non-crystalline form of silicon, making it lightweight and flexible. CdTe technology, known for its cost-effectiveness, employs cadmium telluride layers for efficient energy conversion. CIGS technology combines copper, indium, gallium, and selenium, offering high efficiency and flexible module designs. Each technology has its unique advantages and applications in photovoltaic systems.</p></p>
